Teaching feeds research

Some of our best research cleanup happens right after a class session. When students ask where the intuition breaks, which assumption is unrealistic, or why one design choice matters more than another, they usually point to the exact place where our own explanation is still weak.

What teaching reveals

  • unclear notation,
  • hidden systems assumptions,
  • missing baseline context,
  • and implementation steps that are obvious only to the original author.

Why that helps the lab

Once we fix those explanations for a lecture or tutorial, the same improvement usually carries over to:

  • paper introductions,
  • project READMEs,
  • experiment handoff notes,
  • and code comments in the core pipeline.

A small habit we keep

After each lecture or reading group, we try to write down one question that made us pause. Those questions are often a better guide for future revisions than a generic “polish later” to-do.
